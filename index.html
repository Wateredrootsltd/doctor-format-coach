Doctor Format — Writing Coach
Scene Headings (Sluglines)
Lesson: Where + When. Specific, not poetic.
Where?When?Specificity
Basic:
INT. HOUSE - DAY
Elevated:
INT. VICTORIAN TOWNHOUSE - STUDY - DAY
Variants:
INT./EXT. CAR - MOVING - CONTINUOUS
EXT. DESERT HIGHWAY - LATER
Action Lines (Show, Don’t Tell)
Focus: What → How we see it → Cue the reader. Use an anchoring noun.
Present tense1–3 linesVisible/AudibleAnchoring noun
What is happening (basic):
She enters the room with a sarcastic grin.
How we see it (cinematic):
A toothy grin spreads across her face as the door clicks. She enters the room.
Anchoring noun:
An orchard sprawls across the valley.
An apple sits on a branch.
More examples:
Rain lashes the rooftop. JAX slips, grabs the ledge, hangs on.
A coffee mug trembles on the desk. Footsteps grow closer.
The corridor flickers as the generator struggles.
